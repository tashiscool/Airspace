## Longer Public-Interest Message

Hi [Name],

I’m reaching out because your work touches aviation safety, operations, weather, PIREPs, or air traffic coordination.

I run LeosSoftwareLLC as a donation-only software effort, and I’ve been building Airspace: an open public-interest prototype for fusing aviation weather, aircraft reports, NOTAMs, CARF/ALTRV reservations, USNS-style traffic, route constraints, and workflow state into explainable operational guidance.

The motivating problem is not that pilots lack weather apps. It is that operational systems still do not reliably turn live weather, aircraft reports, forecasts, route impacts, NOTAMs, and ATC constraints into clear guidance fast enough, with source refs and replayable rationale.

The prototype demonstrates:

- affected mission detection,
- weather/PIREP/NOTAM source drilldown,
- route-impact and avoidance summaries,
- relevant PIREP filtering,
- coordination draft generation,
- printable pilot brief handoff,
- decision trace, audit, and replay,
- map overlays that keep CARF/ALTRV, NOTAM, weather, PIREP, route impact, and conflict layers distinct.

This is not certified or operational. I’m asking for public review, not procurement: what is useful, what is wrong, what would be misleading, and what scenarios would make the evaluation more realistic.

## Good Review Targets

- aviation safety researchers,
- NTSB safety-study contacts,
- NASA aviation safety / ATM researchers,
- aviation universities,
- pilot safety foundations,
- dispatch and flight operations educators,
- meteorologists familiar with aviation operations,
- retired controllers or traffic managers,
- open-source aviation/weather communities.

## Avoid As A Primary Path

- unsolicited proprietary commercialization,
- handing core code to a prime contractor,
- proposals that require closed-source control of safety logic,
- claims of FAA approval or operational readiness.
